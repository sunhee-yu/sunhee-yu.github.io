About

I am an Economist at the Bank of Korea. I received my Ph.D. in Economics from The Ohio State University.

My research interests lie in household finance, labor economics, and family economics. I study how households respond to adverse income shocks, credit constraints, and housing market policies, with a particular focus on debt repayment, financial distress, marriage, and fertility decisions.

Research Interests

  • Household Finance
  • Labor Economics
  • Family Economics
  • Housing Economics

Current Research

My current work examines household financial outcomes following unemployment, including delinquency, bankruptcy, and debt repayment behavior around unemployment insurance benefit exhaustion. I also study how housing credit policies affect household formation, with an emphasis on marriage and fertility decisions.
