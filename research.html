Working Papers

Unemployment Insurance Exhaustion and Household Debt Repayment
with Meta Brown

This paper studies how households adjust their debt repayment behavior following job loss and around unemployment insurance benefit exhaustion. Using linked administrative data on unemployment insurance, wages, and consumer credit records, we examine delinquency, bankruptcy, and other measures of financial distress over the unemployment spell.

Housing Credit Policy and Marriage Decisions
This paper examines how housing credit constraints affect household formation. Focusing on changes in loan-to-value (LTV) regulations, the paper studies whether tighter mortgage borrowing constraints delay marriage and fertility, especially among young renters facing greater barriers to home purchase.
